<h1>The debate between 93 unleaded and 95 unleaded and which is cheaper</h1>

<ul>
    <li>The Automobile Association of South Africa suggests that motorists with smaller engines should opt for 93 Unleaded petrol.</li>
    <li>This recommendation challenges the common misconception that 95 Unleaded petrol is more fuel-efficient and cost-effective.</li>
    <li>Opinions among South Africans vary, with some supporting the recommendation while others are not convinced.</li>
</ul>


<img src="../pages/news/news-images copy/aviation/tp-nws5.jpg" alt="Story image">


<p>According to car enthusiast, motorists can save much money using 93-unleaded petrol instead of 95. The Automobile Association of South Africa recommended that those without super or turbochargers rely more on 93-unleaded petrol.</p>

<p>Suzuki South Africa also reportedly explained that cars with smaller engines would not see any difference in fuel efficiency or performance compared to vehicles with larger engines. Even those with larger engines could use 93-unleaded petrol to save money.</p>
